Abstract
Multispectral machine vision enables automated, non-destructive quality inspection, grading, and sorting of horticultural produce. Graphical user interfaces (GUIs) play a critical role in the practical deployment of automated inspection systems by seamlessly integrating hardware controls, real-time inference, and data visualization. Despite significant advancements in imaging technologies and artificial intelligence (AI) for agricultural applications, the lack of flexible, open-sourced software tools creates a barrier to rapid technology iteration and real-world adoption. Building on our prior work, this study presents a modular and user-friendly GUI application for an innovative multispectral vision-based apple sorting system. The GUI is developed using the Qt framework with a modular, multi-threaded architecture. It interfaces with a multispectral camera to acquire and synchronize RGB (red-green-blue) and near infrared imagery in real-time. A dynamic model switching framework is incorporated, allowing users to deploy, evaluate, and compare AI models for fruit quality detection and grading at runtime. Based on the grading output, the GUI controls sorting mechanisms to direct graded fruit to their respective destinations. The software provides a comprehensive visualization dashboard displaying multispectral images, grading statistics, quality summaries, conveyor and sorter status, and conveyor speeds. A dedicated output module allows on-demand logging of image data, grading outcomes, and sorting results. The system performance and reliability of the GUI will be assessed during online, automated apple grading and sorting under varying conveyor speeds. By releasing this GUI as an open-access software tool, this work addresses a critical software gap and provides a valuable resource to accelerate innovations in automated fruit sorting technologies.

Keywords: Apple sorting, Graphical software, Multispectral vision, Postharvest automation
